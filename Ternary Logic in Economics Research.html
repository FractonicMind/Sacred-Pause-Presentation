Implementing Ternary Logic in Economic Decision-Making

Traditional economic models often rely on binary outcomes: buy/sell, growth/recession, success/failure. This infographic explores a more nuanced approach using ternary logic to embrace uncertainty and build more resilient, intelligent systems.

The Third Option: Handling Uncertainty

Ternary logic introduces a third state, 'Indeterminate,' to the traditional 'True' and 'False'. This allows models to explicitly acknowledge and manage incomplete information, a persistent challenge in volatile markets.

TRUE

The condition is met. Data provides high confidence for a 'Yes' or 'Proceed' signal.
(e.g., Asset is undervalued)

FALSE

The condition is not met. Data provides high confidence for a 'No' or 'Halt' signal.
(e.g., Asset is overvalued)

INDETERMINATE

The state is unknown. Data is insufficient, conflicting, or falls within a grey area. Action is deferred pending more information.

Regulatory & Ethical Considerations

Deploying these models requires careful governance to ensure fairness, transparency, and accountability, especially when decisions impact individuals or market stability.

Transparency

How is the 'Indeterminate' state defined and resolved? Stakeholders must understand the criteria that trigger each of the three states.

Accountability

Who is responsible for decisions deferred or made based on an 'Indeterminate' signal? Clear lines of ownership are critical.

Data Bias

Does the training data for the logic engine contain biases that could unfairly place certain outcomes in the 'Indeterminate' category?
